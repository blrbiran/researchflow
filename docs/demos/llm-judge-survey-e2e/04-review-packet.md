# Review Packet

## manuscript_summary
The draft proposes a survey that separates reliability, construct validity, bias, calibration, and governance in LLM-as-a-judge evaluation, then uses that taxonomy to derive conditional guidance.

## critical_issues
- The seed bibliography is not yet a verified or sufficiently broad survey corpus.
- No reproducible review method, inclusion criteria, or extraction protocol is present.

## major_issues
- The taxonomy needs explicit assignment rules so categories do not overlap opportunistically.
- The eventual synthesis must compare studies within sentences and explain contradictions by evaluation condition.
- Practical recommendations must be tied to evidence confidence rather than presented as universal best practices.

## minor_issues
- The opening could later include one concrete example where high average agreement masks a systematic bias.
- The introduction should define “judge” and “human alignment” consistently before using them as comparison terms.

## revision_order
1. Build and verify a broader corpus with explicit search and screening records.
2. Define taxonomy branch boundaries and a structured extraction matrix.
3. Draft the evidence synthesis with contradiction and confidence analysis.
4. Revisit the introduction and practical guidance after the evidence map stabilizes.

## recommended_next_phase
Return to `literature-discovery` rather than continuing downstream: the survey corpus and review method are not yet stable enough for a real synthesis draft.
